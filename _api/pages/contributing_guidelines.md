It is usually not necessary to obtain the permission of a copyright holder before linking to copyrighted material, just as an author of a dead-tree book does not need permission to cite someone else’s work in their bibliography. Likewise, CircuitVerse’s Interactive Book is not restricted to linking only to CC-BY-SA or open-source content.</p>\n\n<hr />\n\n<h2 id=\"proposing-a-contribution\">Proposing a contribution</h2>\n\n<p>If you have in mind new content to be added, it is important that the CircuitVerse community understand why your contribution should be included in the book. You may have already discussed your idea in some of CircuitVerse social channels, in that case, please include the discussion in your proposal.</p>\n\n<p>The proposal should clearly indicate what kind of contribution are you planning to submit (add new content, edit existing content, translate content). When proposing new content, please, also specify whether it will be a whole new chapter, a new section, specific content within a section (text, figures, equations, example circuits, etc.)</p>\n\n<p>Then, include a brief summary of the contribution content followed by the justification why it is needed.</p>\n\n<p>Finaly, submit your proposal to the CircuitVerse Interactive Book coordination team, and discuss the proposal with them in order to refine it. The coordination team will either approve or reject the proposal. In case of rejection, comments will be included explaining why it was rejected, and how could it be improved when the proposed content is useful, but the proposed “format” whas not adequate enough.</p>\n\n<hr />\n\n<h2 id=\"editing-existing-content\">Editing existing content</h2>\n\n<p>When editing existing content, try to contact the original author first in order to discuss your idea.</p>\n\n<p>If the original author cannot be contacted after a “good-faith” effort, fall back to discuss the topic with the CircuiVerse community through any of the social channels.</p>\n\n<hr />\n\n<h2 id=\"writing-content\">Writing content</h2>\n\n<p>All content should be supported by valid sources. Please, research carefuly and validate all your references. Check that there are no copyright or license issues regarding the use of each of your sources.</p>\n\n<p>When using online resources, make a “hard-copy” (screen-capture, pdf-printed version) of them to avoid missing resources in the future (Error 404)</p>\n\n<p>The Interactive Book is not intended as a thorough textbook, but its main focus is on interactive content. Try to keep the content to the amount necessary to understand the topic and cite appropriate references for further study on the subject.</p>\n\n<p>To keep the consistency of the book, please follow the style indicated in these guidelines, also use the templates and existing content as example.</p>\n\n<h3 id=\"quality-assurance\">Quality assurance</h3>\n\n<p>Besides researching and validating your sources and adhering to the Interactive Book style, please also edit (remove unnecesary content) and check for spelling and grammar errors. Then, forward your contribution to a third party for proofreading. When you are satisfied with the quality and accuracy of your contribution ask for peer reviewers from the CircuitVerse community. Finally, submit your work to the “formal” review by creating a pull-request of your forked repository</p>\n\n<hr />\n\n<h2 id=\"style-manual\">Style manual</h2>\n\n<p>[Work in Progress]</p>\n\n<p>While most of the formatting style is carried out automatically by the layout, templates and stylesheets in the SSG (jekyll) pipeline, it is important to also adhere to a common writing style in order to assure consistency of the book.</p>\n\n<p>Please use a neutral and simple language. Avoid slang. Also, technical jargon should be explained.</p>\n\n<h3 id=\"figures-and-tables\">Figures and tables</h3>\n\n<p>Figures and tables should include a description or caption, and they should be commented in text. If a figure or table is not part of the description, probably it is not needed and can be removed from the book.</p>\n\n<p>See an example in section <a href=\"#templates-and-examples\">Templates and examples</a>.</p>\n\n<h3 id=\"equations\">Equations</h3>\n\n<p>Equations can be typesetted in LaTeX to be rendered by <a href=\"https://www.mathjax.org/\">MathJax</a>. A few examples can be found in the <a href=\"#templates-and-examples\">Templates and examples</a> section. If equations need to be referenced in the text, use numbered or labelled equations. General useful guidelines:</p>\n\n<ul>\n  <li>There are many variants for the notation of negated variables, prefer the bar over the variable or the <em>prime</em> (complement) symbol.</li>\n  <li>For binary words with many literals (high number of bits) prefer a single letter with subscript numbers for each bit. For instance a four bit word can be written as $(x_3,x_2,x_1,x_0)$ instead of a different letter for each literal $(A,B,C,D)$. The least significant bit should be subscripted with 0. In case of ambiguity or if the opposite arrangement is necessary, use a superscript mark to identify the MSB and LSB: $(x_0^{\\text{MSB}},x_1,x_2,x_3^{\\text{LSB}})$</li>\n</ul>\n\n<h3 id=\"references\">References</h3>\n\n<p>Information obtained from other sources should be properly cited and referenced. The top-level is for chapters and the second level is for sections in a chapter</p>\n\n<h3 id=\"new-chapter-template\">New chapter template</h3>\n\n<p>Each chapter should be a directory under the <code class=\"language-plaintext highlighter-rouge\">docs</code> directory. The <code class=\"language-plaintext highlighter-rouge\">index.md</code> in the chapter directory is used for the Chapter text and table of contents.</p>\n\n<p>The <code class=\"language-plaintext highlighter-rouge\">index.md</code> file should have the following contents:</p>\n\n<div class=\"language-markdown highlighter-rouge\"><div class=\"highlight\"><pre class=\"highlight\"><code><span class=\"nn\">---</span>\n<span class=\"na\">layout</span><span class=\"pi\">:</span> <span class=\"s\">circuitverse</span>\n<span class=\"na\">title</span><span class=\"pi\">:</span> <span class=\"s\">Chapter title</span>\n<span class=\"na\">nav_order</span><span class=\"pi\">:</span> <span class=\"m\">10</span>\n<span class=\"na\">has_children</span><span class=\"pi\">:</span> <span class=\"no\">true</span>\n<span class=\"na\">has_toc</span><span class=\"pi\">:</span> <span class=\"no\">false</span>\n<span class=\"nn\">---</span>\n\n<span class=\"gh\"># Long chapter title</span>\n{: .no_toc}\n\nIntroductory chapter text\n\n<span class=\"gu\">## Chapter contents</span>\n{: .no_toc .text-delta}<span class=\"sb\">\n\n\n</span>{% include chapter_toc.html %}\n\n</code></pre></div></div>\n\n<p>The <code class=\"language-plaintext highlighter-rouge\">title</code> and <code class=\"language-plaintext highlighter-rouge\">nav_order</code> elements in the front matter should be filled-in accordingly. The other elements in the front matter block should be left as in the template above.</p>\n\n<p>After the “Long” chapter title an introductory text for the chapter can be added. It is followed by the Table of contents’ title and the include directive which create a list of links to the sections of the chapter according to their level and navigation order.</p>\n\n<h3 id=\"new-section-template\">New section template</h3>\n\n<p>Files for sections within a chapter must be placed in the chapter’s directory. Currently, the contents of the book are organised in three levels of difficulty:</p>\n\n<ol>\n  <li>basic</li>\n  <li>medium</li>\n  <li>advanced</li>\n</ol>\n\n<p>The following code represents an empty section and can be used as a template</p>\n\n<div class=\"language-markdown highlighter-rouge\"><div class=\"highlight\"><pre class=\"highlight\"><code><span class=\"nn\">---</span>\n<span class=\"na\">layout</span><span class=\"pi\">:</span> <span class=\"s\">circuitverse</span>\n<span class=\"na\">title</span><span class=\"pi\">:</span> <span class=\"s\">Section title</span>\n<span class=\"na\">nav_order</span><span class=\"pi\">:</span> <span class=\"s\">l0s000</span>\n<span class=\"na\">cvib_level</span><span class=\"pi\">:</span> <span class=\"s\">advanced</span>\n<span class=\"na\">parent</span><span class=\"pi\">:</span> <span class=\"s\">Parent Chapter</span>\n<span class=\"na\">has_children</span><span class=\"pi\">:</span> <span class=\"no\">false</span>\n<span class=\"nn\">---</span>\n\n<span class=\"gh\"># Long section title</span>\n{: .no_toc}\n\n<span class=\"gu\">## Table of contents</span>\n{: .no_toc .text-delta}\n<span class=\"p\">\n1.</span> TOC\n{:toc}\n\n<span class=\"gu\">## Title</span>\n\nText\n</code></pre></div></div>\n\n<p>Each section file should have content belonging only to one of these levels. Therefore, the front matter block presents the <code class=\"language-plaintext highlighter-rouge\">cvib_level</code> which correspond to one of the levels.</p>\n\n<p>The <code class=\"language-plaintext highlighter-rouge\">parent</code> element must be the name of the Chapter containing the section.</p>\n\n<p>The section’s title goes in the <code class=\"language-plaintext highlighter-rouge\">title</code> element.</p>\n\n<p>To standardise the order of sections in the navigation bar according to the difficulty level, the <code class=\"language-plaintext highlighter-rouge\">nav_order</code> element is an “encoded” string with this form: <code class=\"language-plaintext highlighter-rouge\">l</code>, followed by a digit, followed by <code class=\"language-plaintext highlighter-rouge\">s</code>, followed by three digits. <code class=\"language-plaintext highlighter-rouge\">l</code> stands for <em>level</em> and is a number between 0 and 2 and represent the basic, medium and advanced level, respectively. <code class=\"language-plaintext highlighter-rouge\">s</code> stands for <em>section</em> and the three digit number after it is a zero-padded number indicating the section position in the chapter.</p>\n\n<p>The other elements in the front matter block (<code class=\"language-plaintext highlighter-rouge\">layout</code>, <code class=\"language-plaintext highlighter-rouge\">has_children</code>) should be should be left as they are.</p>\n\n<p>After the front matter block comes the section’s title followed by the table of contents title and code to generate it automatically.</p>\n\n<p>After that, parts within the section can be added.</p>\n\n<h3 id=\"equations-examples\">Equations examples</h3>\n\n<p>Equations are
